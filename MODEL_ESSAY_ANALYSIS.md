#### 3. **Strong Organization**
- Clear paragraph structure

#### 4. **Appropriate Word Count**

#### 5. **Useful Highlights**
The "highlights" feature effectively points out:
- Academic vocabulary used

### Minor Issues Found

#### 1. **Occasional Repetitive Transitions**
Some essays use the same linking phrase multiple times:
- "For instance" + "For example" + "Additionally" pattern is common
- Could vary more with "To illustrate," "Specifically," "In particular"

**Recommendation**: Add more variety in linking words within essays

#### 2. **Some Formulaic Opening Lines**
A few essays start with similar patterns:
- "X has become..." appears frequently
- "In recent years..." is common
- "There is debate about..." is repeated

**Recommendation**: Show students multiple ways to open essays

#### 3. **Word Count Accuracy**
Previously identified: 11 files have claimed vs. actual word count mismatches (minor ±6-10 words)

**Recommendation**: Update claimed word counts to match actual counts

## ACADEMIC PHRASES & LINKING WORDS ANALYSIS

### Current State

Most files provide good linking phrase categories:
- **Introducing ideas**: 3-4 options
- **Contrasting**: 3-4 options
- **Adding information**: 3-4 options
- **Giving examples**: 3-4 options
- **Showing results**: 3-4 options
- **Concluding**: 3-4 options

### Recommendations for Improvement

#### 1. **Expand Linking Phrase Options**
Current: Most categories have 3-4 options
**Recommendation**: Expand to 5-6 options per category

**Example additions**:

**For Contrasting** (current has 3-4):
- Add: "That said," "Nonetheless," "In spite of this"

**For Exemplifying** (current has 3-4):
- Add: "To illustrate this point," "A case in point," "Notably"

**For Showing Cause/Effect** (some files missing):
- Add category: "Due to," "As a consequence of," "This stems from"

#### 2. **Add "Qualifying/Hedging" Category**
Many IELTS essays benefit from hedging language. Add section:
- "It could be argued that"
- "To some extent"
- "In many cases"
- "Generally speaking"
- "By and large"

#### 3. **Add "Emphasizing" Category**
For strengthening arguments:
- "It is crucial that"
- "Particularly important is"
- "Above all"
- "Significantly"
- "Notably"

**Files WITHOUT**: 61 files (87%)

### Why Sentence Unscramble Exercises Are Valuable

1. **Reinforce Word Order**: Helps students internalize English syntax patterns
2. **Build Confidence**: Lower stakes practice before full sentence writing
3. **Highlight Collocations**: Shows which words naturally go together
4. **Active Learning**: More engaging than passive vocabulary review
5. **Quick Assessment**: Students can quickly check understanding

### Recommendation: ADD Sentence Unscramble to All Files

**Suggested Quantity**: 5-6 exercises per topic (matching files that already have them)

**Quality Guidelines**:
- Use vocabulary from the topic
- Include key linking words
- Show varied sentence structures
- Range from simple to complex
- Include at least one conditional or relative clause

**Example Good Sentences to Include**:

**Simple** (7-8 words):
- "Social media has transformed how people communicate globally."
- "Environmental protection requires collective action from all sectors."

**Medium** (10-12 words):
- "Governments can implement regulations to reduce industrial pollution significantly in manufacturing sectors."
- "Remote work enables employees to balance their professional and personal commitments more effectively."

**Complex** (12-15 words):
- "Although remote work offers flexibility, it can lead to social isolation if not managed properly."
- "Research suggests that teenagers who spend excessive time on social media experience higher anxiety levels."
